Abstract

Although recent research on single-view 3D reconstruction has yielded notable progress, existing methodologies still face challenges in accurately reconstructing complex and large-scale outdoor environments. In this work, we present PanoramicSplat, a novel view synthesis model based on 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), featuring three key innovations designed for monocular large-scale scene reconstruction. First, we introduce Panoramic Field Supervision, a self-supervised paradigm that resolves single-view ambiguities through cyclic view consistency. Second, we develop a Semantic-aware Depth Regularization mechanism that injects scene semantic and depth priors into Gaussian primitives optimization to enhance the structural understanding of the scene. Third, we introduce a Radially Weighted Photometric Loss coupled with a Gaussian Pruning mechanism that emphasizes central regions and suppresses redundant primitives. To support our research, we constructed a comprehensive dataset of large-scale outdoor scenes using Google Earth Studio, capturing diverse urban landscapes and occluded structures. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach significantly outperforms existing monocular methods in qualitative benchmarks.
